This app has had a lot of indirect help:
  • The word "trail" came from Tom Sheldon and his book of Sudokus, which my wife bought for me years ago.
  • Peter Norvig's brilliant Python code for solving Sudoku informs a great deal of the core library behind this app.
  • I mined David Bau's Chrome extension Heidi's Sudoku Hintpad for ideas for the solver and the generator.
  • The icons in this app mostly come from an awesome site called The Noun Project. As per their attribution rules, we list and link to the icons and their designers below.
  • More validation for the trail approach, and for improper puzzles, came from J. F. Crook's AMS paper (PDF).

The icons

  • Arrow designed by Mateo Zlatar from The Noun Project (used for Undo, Redo, Undo to Start, and Redo to End)
  • Trail by an unknown designer, 1982 (used for New Trail)
  • Play designed by Mateo Zlatar from The Noun Project (used for Play and Reverse on the Solution page)
  • Pause designed by Mateo Zlatar from The Noun Project (used on the Solution page)
  • Wipe designed by Johan H. W. Basberg (used for Clear Insights on the Solution page)
  • Pin designed by KB (used for Apply Selected Insight on the Solution page)
  • Loop designed by P.J. Onori from The Noun Project (for Resume Replay on the Solution page)
